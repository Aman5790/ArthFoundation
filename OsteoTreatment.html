Risk Factor Reduction

  • Supervision in cases of steroids & thyroid hormone replacement Anti Epilepsy & antacid therapy
  • Cessation of smoking and alcohol intake
  • Eye-sight correction to reduce falls
  • Hip protectors to avoid hip fracture in elderly
  • Avoid exposed wires, slippery rugs, loose carpets etc inside the home.

Nutritional Recommendations

  • Calcium
    1. Optimal calcium intake reduces bone loss and suppresses bone turnover
    Life Stage Group Estimated Adequate Daily Calcium Intake, mg/d Young children (1–3 years) 500 Older children (4–8 years) 800 Adolescents and young adults (9–18 years) 1300 Men and women (19–50 years) 1000 Men and women (51 and older) 1200
  • Vitamin D
    1. The Institute of Medicine recommends daily intakes-
    Life Stage Group Estimated Adequate Daily Calcium Intake, mg/d Adults- (below 50 Yrs) 200IU Elderly- (50-70 Yrs) 400IU 70 Yrs and above 600IU
    1. Note: Once a month tablet 60 Thousand units for maintenance therapy available in market

Surgical Treatment

  • Fragility Fractures – commonly around –Wrist, Hip, Pelvis, Spine.
  • Some times the fracture union not possible with conservative treatment like POP then surgical interventions available
  • For more info - please visit AFI website: www.arthfound.org
