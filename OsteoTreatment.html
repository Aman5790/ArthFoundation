Treatment

Treatment for Osteoporosis needs

  1. Risk factor reduction
  2. Nutritional recommendations
  3. Pharmacological therapies
  4. Exercise and Psychological Support
  5. Medicinal
  6. Surgical treatment

Risk Factor Reduction

  • Supervision in case of steroid and thyroid hormones intake, replacement Anti Epilepsy and antacid therapy
  • Cessation of smoking and minimize alcohol intake
  • Eye-sight correction to reduce falls
  • Hip protectors to avoid hip fracture in elderly
  • Avoid exposed wires, slippery rugs, loose carpets etc inside the home.

Nutritional Recommendations

  • Calcium
    1. Optimal calcium intake reduces bone loss and suppresses bone turnover
    Life Stage Group Estimated Adequate Daily Calcium Intake, mg/d Young children (1–3 years) 500 Older children (4–8 years) 800 Adolescents and young adults (9–18 years) 1300 Men and women (19–50 years) 1000 Men and women (51 and older) 1200
  • Vitamin D
    1. Institute of nutrition-
    Age group Daily vitamin requirement Adults- (below 50 Yrs) 200IU Elderly- (50-70 Yrs) 400IU 70 Yrs and above 600IU
    1. Note: Once a month tablet 60 Thousand units for maintenance therapy available in market

Exercise and Psychological Support

  1. Exercise
    1. Weight-bearing exercise – walking, jogging, field games
    2. Maintains and builds up bone mass
    3. Better balance
    4. Fall prevention
  2. Psychological support
    1. Counseling for sustained Osteoporosis treatment (to avoid dropouts)
    2. Rehabilitation- mainly for spine deformities and fragility fractures

Medicinal - This treatment is simple but to be taken after doctor’s advice

  1. Mild
    1. Adequate calcium and vitamin D intake
    2. Weight-bearing exercises- walking / field games
    3. Avoid under nutrition and protein malnutrition
    4. Adequate supply of vitamin D, sufficient sun exposure / oral supplementation
    5. Increase the level of physical activity
    6. Avoid smoking
    7. Avoid heavy drinking
  2. Moderate
    1. Calcium – dose mentioned above
    2. Vitamin-D – dose mentioned above
    3. Antiresorptive Drugs – Bisphosphonates like Tab Alandronate 10mg daily, Tab Ibandronate , Tab Risodronate 75mg weekly or 150mg monthly
    4. Slective Estrogen receptor modulators (SERMS) – like Tab Raloxifene
    5. Calcitonin – Intranasal spray (one puff alternate nastral daily for a period of 3 or 6 months)
  3. Severe
    1. One forming agents like parathyroid Hormone (Teriparatide) subcutaneous injection for one and half years
    2. Fragility fractures usually at - Wrist, Spine, Hip, Shoulder. (Conservative or Surgical treatment as per advice of Orthopedic Surgeon)

Surgical Treatment

  • Mainly for fragility fractures – commonly around –Wrist, Hip, Pelvis, Spine.
  • Some times the fracture union not possible with conservative treatment like POP/fibre cast then surgical interventions available
